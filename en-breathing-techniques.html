Control Your Breath, Control Your Mind

Breathing techniques help calm the airways and reduce panic during mild shortness of breath.

Breathe

Follow the rhythm of the circle to calm down

👄

Pursed Lip Breathing

Highly effective for asthma patients to keep the airways open longer.

  1. 1

    Inhale slowly through your nose (2-second count).

  2. 2

    Pucker your lips as if you are about to blow out a candle.

  3. 3

    Exhale slowly through your pursed lips (4-second count).

🧘

Diaphragmatic Breathing (Belly Breathing)

This technique helps you utilize your lungs to their maximum capacity.

  • Place one hand on your chest and the other on your belly.
  • Ensure your belly moves outward as you inhale.

Expert Tip:

Practice this for 5-10 minutes daily to strengthen your lungs over the long term.

⚠️

Warning

Breathing exercises are not a replacement for a reliever inhaler. If you are experiencing a severe asthma attack, use your medication first and seek medical help.
