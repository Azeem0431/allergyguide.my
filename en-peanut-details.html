Peanut Allergy

Peanut allergy is one of the leading causes of severe allergic reactions (anaphylaxis). Unlike some other allergies, peanut allergies are typically lifelong.

Where are Peanuts Commonly Found?

Obvious Sources

  • • Peanut butter
  • • Mixed nuts / Trail mix
  • • Cold-pressed peanut oil

Hidden Sources

  • • Satay sauce and peanut gravy
  • • Asian cuisines (Thai, Chinese, Malay)
  • • Chocolates, biscuits, and pastries
  • • Meat alternative / vegetarian protein dishes

Emergency Red Flags

🫁

Respiratory

Constriction of airways, severe shortness of breath, or persistent coughing.

👄

Rapid Swelling

Visible swelling of the tongue, lips, and throat causing difficulty swallowing.

🧠

Nervous System

Severe dizziness, feeling like fainting, or loss of consciousness.

❤️

Cardiac

Pulse becoming weak or suddenly very rapid.

Myth vs. Fact

Myth

"Peanut oil is always dangerous for those with an allergy."

Fact: Highly refined peanut oil is usually safe because the allergic proteins have been removed. However, gourmet or cold-pressed oils are extremely dangerous.

Myth

"Smelling the scent of peanuts can cause anaphylaxis."

Fact: Most reactions occur through contact or ingestion. However, peanut dust (e.g., in a factory) can trigger a reaction if inhaled.

Action Plan: If a Reaction Occurs

  1. 01

    Use EpiPen: If you have an epinephrine auto-injector, use it immediately on the outer mid-thigh.

  2. 02

    Call Emergency (999): Even if symptoms seem to subside, you must go to the hospital as a secondary reaction can occur.

  3. 03

    Lie Down & Elevate Legs: This helps maintain blood flow to the heart and brain while waiting for assistance.
