Tree Nut Allergy

Distinct from peanuts (which are legumes), tree nuts grow on trees. If you are allergic to one type of tree nut, you are at high risk of being allergic to other types as well.

1 Main Types of Tree Nuts

Almond

Cashew

Walnut

Hazelnut

Pistachio

Pecan

Macadamia

Brazil Nut

Beware of These Products

  • • Nutella / Chocolate Spreads: Contains Hazelnuts.
  • • Pesto Sauce: Traditionally uses Pine Nuts.
  • • Marzipan: A sweet paste made from Almonds.
  • • Nut Oils: Walnut or almond oils used in salads.
  • • Flavor Extracts: Artificial vs. natural almond extract.
  • • Energy Bars / Granola: A major source of tree nuts.
  • • Alternative Milks: Almond, cashew, or macadamia milk.
  • • Personal Care Products: Lotions or shampoos with almond oil/shea butter.

Note for Malaysians

While Peanuts are more common in daily local cuisine, Cashews are often used in kurma dishes, certain rendangs, or served as premium snacks. If dining at high-end restaurants or bakeries, always inquire about tree nut blends used in cakes and pastries.

Tips for Tree Nut Allergy Patients:

🚨

Avoid 'Bulk Bins': Do not buy food from open containers at supermarkets (like self-weigh sections) due to the extremely high risk of cross-contamination via shared scoops.

🏥

EpiPen is Mandatory: Tree nut allergies are known triggers for very rapid anaphylaxis. Always carry your emergency medication supply.

📖

Read Cosmetic Labels: Some high-quality body lotions use tree nut oils that can cause reactions on sensitive skin.
