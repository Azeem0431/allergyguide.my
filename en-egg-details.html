Egg Allergy

An egg allergy occurs when the body's immune system overreacts to proteins found in egg whites or egg yolks. It is the second most common food allergy in children.

1 Egg White vs Egg Yolk

Those with an egg allergy must avoid both components because the risk of protein cross-contamination is extremely high.

2 Where is Egg Hidden?

Bakery Products & Sauces

  • • Cakes, muffins, and cookies.
  • • Mayonnaise and salad dressings.
  • • Fresh pasta & Egg noodles.
  • • Meatballs (as a binder).

Label Terms (Be Careful)

  • • Albumin (Primary protein).
  • • Lecithin (If source is unspecified).
  • • Lysozyme (Found in cheese).
  • • Meringue & Surimi.
