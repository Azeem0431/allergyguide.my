Treatment & Prevention Guide

Fast and accurate steps to manage your allergies wisely.

🚨 Rapid Anaphylaxis Action

If a victim experiences shortness of breath, facial swelling, or fainting after exposure to an allergen:

  • ✅ Inject EpiPen (If available)
  • ✅ Lay victim flat & lift legs
  • ✅ Call 999 immediately
  • ✅ Do not give water or oral medicine

Home Remedies

🚿

Cold Shower / Cold Compress

Cold temperatures constrict blood vessels and reduce histamine release under the skin. Highly effective for hives.

🍯

Pure Stingless Bee Honey (Kelulut)

Possesses natural anti-inflammatory properties. Helps reduce sinus symptoms if consumed consistently (Ensure no pollen allergy).

💦

Nasal Rinse

Physically removes allergens like dust and pollen from the nasal cavity. Use clean distilled water.

Pharmacology (Medications)

Type of Medicine Main Function
Antihistamines (e.g., Loratadine, Cetirizine) Stops itching reactions, sneezing, and watery eyes.
Decongestants (e.g., Oxymetazoline) Relieves nasal congestion quickly.
Topical Corticosteroids (External Creams) Treats red and thickened skin inflammation.

3-Step Prevention Strategy

🔍

1. Identify

Undergo blood tests (RAST) or Skin Prick Tests at a hospital to determine the exact cause of your allergies.

🚫

2. Avoid

For food allergies, read ingredient labels. For dust allergies, use anti-dust mite mattress covers.

📢

3. Inform

Notify schools, workplaces, or restaurant servers about your allergies for your own safety.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can allergies be fully cured? ▼

Allergies cannot be totally "cured," but they can be managed. Some children may outgrow milk or egg allergies as they reach adulthood.

What is the difference between Allergy and Intolerance? ▼

Allergy involves the immune system (can be fatal). Intolerance (like lactose) only involves the digestive system (usually bloating or diarrhea).

Preparing to Meet a Specialist

Prepare this information before your doctor's appointment:

RECORD 1

Date & time the reaction started.

RECORD 2

Food list from the last 24 hours.

RECORD 3

Clear photos of rashes/swelling.

RECORD 4

List of medications or herbs taken.

Still Need Help?

Don't let allergies disrupt your quality of life. Get advice from a certified immunologist or dermatologist.
