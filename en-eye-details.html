Red & Itchy Eyes?

Learn the difference between common eye infections and ocular allergies for the right treatment.

👁️

🧬 Types of Eye Allergies

Seasonal Allergic Conjunctivitis (SAC)

The most common type. Triggered by airborne pollens that vary according to the season.

Perennial Allergic Conjunctivitis (PAC)

Occurs year-round. Usually caused by dust mites, mold, or pet dander.

Contact Allergic Conjunctivitis

Caused by irritation from contact lenses or chemical substances in cosmetics and eye drops.

Identify Your Symptoms

✓ Extreme Itchiness
✓ Gritty sensation (Sand in eyes)
✓ Light Sensitivity (Glare)
✓ Swollen Eyelids

Quick Care Guide

DO: Best Practices

  • • Wash face & hands after being outdoors.
  • • Use preservative-free artificial tears (Lubricants).
  • • Wear sunglasses when outside.

DON'T: Avoid

  • • Do not rub your eyes! It breaks mast cells & releases histamine.
  • • Avoid contact lenses while eyes are red/irritated.
  • • Never share face towels with others.
👓

Contact Lens Wearer?

Allergen proteins can stick to the lens surface. If eyes are itchy, switch to glasses immediately until symptoms subside.

GPC ALERT

Cold Compress Tip

Place a metal spoon in the fridge for 10 minutes, then press the back of the spoon against swollen eyelids. It works instantly!

When to see a Specialist?

  • • Your vision becomes blurry.
  • • You feel severe pain inside the eyeball.
  • • Thick pus discharge (not regular crusting).
