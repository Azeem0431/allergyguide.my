Cow's Milk Allergy

Cow's milk allergy is one of the most common food allergies among infants and young children. It involves an immune system reaction to milk proteins (Casein and Whey).

Allergy vs. Lactose Intolerance

Milk Allergy

Involves the immune system. The body rejects milk proteins. Can cause anaphylaxis and severe skin rashes.

Lactose Intolerance

Involves the digestive system. The body struggles to digest milk sugar (lactose). Causes bloating and diarrhea only.

2 Where is Milk Protein Found?

Common Foods

  • • Butter, Cheese, and Yogurt.
  • • Ice Cream and Pudding.
  • • Milk Chocolate.
  • • Coffee Creamer (even some non-dairy creamers contain milk protein!).

Labels to Watch For

  • • Casein / Caseinate.
  • • Whey (milk serum).
  • • Lactalbumin / Lactose.
  • • Ghee (clarified butter).
  • • Artificial Butter Flavor.

Symptoms in Infants

🤮

Frequent Vomiting

🩹

Severe Eczema

💩

Bloody Stools

😭

Colic (Excessive Crying)

Safe Alternatives (Non-Dairy Milk):

Soy Milk

Most popular alternative, high in protein.

Almond / Oat Milk

Great for cooking and coffee beverages.

Rice Milk

Safest choice if the child is also allergic to soy.
