What is Anaphylaxis?

A severe, life-threatening allergic reaction that occurs suddenly (within minutes) and can be fatal if not treated immediately.

! Warning Signs (Checklist)

🫁

Breathing

Shortness of breath, wheezing, or persistent coughing.

👅

Swelling

Swollen tongue, tight throat, or hoarse voice.

🧠

Consciousness

Dizziness, feeling faint, or confusion.

🤢

Stomach

Persistent vomiting or severe abdominal pain.

Immediate Actions

1

Call 999

Tell the operator: "Anaphylaxis Allergy Emergency".

2

Use EpiPen (If available)

Inject into the outer mid-thigh. Hold for 10 seconds.

3

Lay the Patient Down

Lay them flat. Elevate the legs. Do NOT allow the patient to stand or walk.

4

Monitor Breathing

If unconscious and no pulse is felt, begin CPR if you are trained.

WARNING: DO NOT DO THIS

  • ❌ Do not give the patient food or drink.
  • ❌ Do not put a pillow under their head (this can close the airway).
  • ❌ Do not wait to "see how it goes" — act now!
