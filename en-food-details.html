Food Allergy

A food allergy is an immune system reaction that occurs soon after eating a certain food. Even a tiny amount of the allergy-causing food can trigger signs and symptoms such as digestive problems, hives, or swollen airways.

Levels of Allergy Symptoms

Mild to Moderate

  • Itchy hives or skin rash
  • Red, itchy, or watery eyes
  • Swelling of the lips or tongue
  • Stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting

Emergency (Anaphylaxis)

  • Shortness of breath or wheezing
  • Sudden drop in blood pressure
  • Weak and rapid pulse
  • Fainting or loss of consciousness

2 Hidden Dangers

Cross-Contamination

This occurs when safe food comes into contact with allergens via shared utensils, fryers (using the same oil), or cutting boards.

Hidden Allergen Names

Milk may be labeled as Casein or Whey. Eggs may appear as Albumin. Always be aware of technical ingredient terms.

Allergy Management Strategies

A

Always Be Prepared

Carry antihistamines or an EpiPen (if prescribed) wherever you go.

B

Emergency Action Plan

Ensure family members, friends, or colleagues know how to act if you suffer an anaphylactic attack.

C

Regular Check-ups

Schedule yearly blood tests (IgE) or Skin Prick Tests with an immunologist to monitor your status.
