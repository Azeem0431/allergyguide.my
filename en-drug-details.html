Drug Allergy:
Identify & Prevent

Drug allergies can happen to anyone, at any time. It is not just a common side effect, but a serious immune system reaction.

DRUGS

⚖️ Allergy vs Side Effect

Side Effects

Expected and common.
Example: Drowsiness after cold medicine or stomach upset after antibiotics.

Drug Allergy

Unexpected immune reaction.
Example: Itchy rash (Hives), swollen lips, or shortness of breath (Anaphylaxis).

When Will Reactions Appear?

Immediate

Minutes to Hours

Usually anaphylaxis, hives, or breathing difficulty. Occurs immediately after ingestion or injection.

Delayed

Hours to Days

Standard red rashes or itching. Sometimes takes 2-3 days to fully manifest.

Very Late

1 to 3 Weeks

Serious reactions like SJS or DRESS. Involves fever and internal organ damage.

💡 Drug Allergy: Myths vs Facts

MYTH

"If I wasn't allergic to this medicine before, I will never be allergic to it."

FACT

Allergies can develop at any time, even if you have taken the same medication before without issues.

MYTH

"Herbal or traditional medicines do not cause allergies."

FACT

Natural ingredients can also trigger severe immune reactions in certain individuals.

How is an Allergy Confirmed?

Don't guess. An Allergy Specialist (Allergist) will conduct clinical tests to confirm your allergy type:

  • Skin Prick Test
  • Patch Test
  • Blood Test (Specific IgE)

Important Note!

"Only perform allergy tests at hospitals or specialist clinics equipped with emergency resuscitation kits. Never attempt self-testing at home!"

High-Risk Medications

🧫

Penicillin Group

The most common cause of antibiotic allergies in Malaysia.

💊

NSAIDs (Painkillers)

Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Naproxen, and Mefenamic Acid (Ponstan).

⚡

Anticonvulsants

Examples: Carbamazepine and Phenytoin.

🧪

Gout Medication

Example: Allopurinol (can cause severe skin reactions).

Dangerous Skin Reactions (SCAR)

Seek immediate hospital care if you develop a rash accompanied by blisters and high fever after taking a new medication:

SJS / TEN

Stevens-Johnson Syndrome

DRESS Syndrome

Organ Failure Risk

⚠️

Allergy Card

If you have a drug allergy, you MUST carry an allergy card or have it recorded in the MySejahtera app.

Safety Tips:

  • ✅ Always keep a photo of the medication you are allergic to on your phone.
  • ✅ Ask your doctor if new medications have 'cross-reactivity' with your known allergies.
  • ✅ Never buy unlabeled medications or 'mixed' pills from unauthorized vendors.

Cross-Reactivity

"Sometimes if you are allergic to one drug, your body may react to other drugs within the same chemical family."

🚨 Immediate Actions if Allergic

  1. Stop taking the medication immediately.
  2. Do not wait for symptoms to worsen; take photos of the rash/swelling for the doctor.
  3. Seek medical help at the nearest Clinic or Emergency Room (A&E).
  4. Bring the suspected medication or its packaging with you.
