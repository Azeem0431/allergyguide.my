Antihistamines (Oral)

Blocks histamine from triggering allergic reactions.

First Generation
(Sedating)

CAUSES DROWSINESS

Chlorpheniramine (Piriton)

Effective for itchy skin & severe sneezing. Lasts 4-6 hours.

Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)

Commonly used for acute allergies and as a sleep aid.

Newer Generation
(Non-Drowsy)

PRIMARY CHOICE
  • Cetirizine (Zyrtec) Most potent
  • Loratadine (Clarityne) 24-hour duration
  • Fexofenadine (Telfast) Very safe
  • Bilastine (Bilaxten) Latest Technology
👃

Nasal Sprays

Best for sinusitis, morning sneezing, and nasal congestion.

Spray Type Example Brands Main Function
Intranasal Steroids Nasonex, Flixonase, Avamys Reduces chronic inflammation. Requires daily use.
Decongestants Oxymetazoline (Iliadin) Use for a maximum of 5 days only! (Avoid 'Rebound Congestion')
Saline Spray Sterimar, NeilMed Rinses allergens/dust from the nasal cavity. Very safe.
👁️

Eye Drops

Anti-Histamine Eye Drops

Example: Ketotifen (Zaditen). Rapidly stops eye itching.

Mast Cell Stabilizers

Example: Crolom. Prevents cells from releasing allergy chemicals.

Artificial Tears

Helps flush pollen out of eyes & lubricates stinging eyes.

🧴

Topicals (Creams & Ointments)

For Eczema, Hives, and Skin Inflammation.

Steroid Creams (Corticosteroids)

Example: Hydrocortisone, Mometasone (Elocon), Betamethasone.

GUIDE: Apply a thin layer only on red areas. Do not use for more than 2 weeks on the face.

Calamine Lotion

Best for relieving itchiness from insect bites or hives.

Steroid Potency Ranking:

  • Low Potency: Hydrocortisone 1%
  • Medium Potency: Eumovate
  • High Potency: Elocon / Betnovate
  • Ultra High: Dermovate

*Use only as directed by a dermatologist!

Epinephrine Injection (EpiPen)

The only medication that can save a life during an Anaphylaxis (fatal allergic reaction) attack.

1

Remove the blue safety cap.

2

Swing and push the orange tip firmly against outer thigh (works through clothing).

3

Hold for 10 seconds and go to the hospital immediately!

When to Use?

  • • Throat swelling & difficulty swallowing.
  • • Wheezing or severe shortness of breath.
  • • Rapid heartbeat & feeling faint.
  • • Sudden drop in blood pressure.

IMPORTANT: DO NOT SHARE MEDICATION

Every body has different sensitivities. Medication that works for your friend might be dangerous for you. Always consult a licensed pharmacist.
