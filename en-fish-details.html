Fish Allergy

Unlike many other allergies that begin in childhood, fish allergies often develop during adulthood. It is primarily caused by the protein **parvalbumin** found in fish muscle.

! Crucial Fish Allergy Facts

Fish vs. Shellfish

Having a fish allergy does not necessarily mean you are allergic to shellfish (shrimp/crab). However, the risk of cross-contamination at markets and restaurants is extremely high.

All Types of Fish?

Most people allergic to one type of fish (e.g., Salmon) are usually advised to avoid all species of finned fish due to similar protein structures.

Beware of Hidden Ingredients

  • Fish Sauce / Budu: Staples in Asian cuisine.
  • Worcestershire Sauce: Often contains anchovies.
  • Fish Gelatin: Sometimes found in gummy candies or vitamins.
  • Omega-3 Supplements: Usually derived from fish oil.
  • Surimi / Imitation Crab: Made from processed white fish paste.
  • Caesar Dressing: Frequently contains anchovy paste.

Safety Precautions

🍲

Airborne Contamination

Fish proteins can be released into the air through steam when fish is boiled or fried. If your allergy is severe, avoid being in the kitchen while fish is being cooked.

🛒

Market Cross-Contact

Avoid purchasing meat or poultry that is displayed directly next to the fish counter or uses the same ice beds.
