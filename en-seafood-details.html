Seafood Allergy

Seafood allergies are divided into two main categories. Click the categories below to view a more detailed list of allergens.

A Understanding Allergen Types (Click for details)

1. Shellfish

Most frequent trigger for serious reactions.

  • • Crustaceans: Shrimp, Crab, Lobster.
  • • Mollusks: Clams, Squid, Oysters, Snails.

2. Finned Fish

A single protein (parvalbumin) is the cause.

  • • Salmon, Tuna, Cod, Mackerel.
  • • Fish-based products (Fish sauce, shrimp paste/belacan).

Symptoms of Seafood Allergy

Skin

Hives, severe itching, and skin turning red/warm.

Digestive

Stomach cramps, diarrhea, and persistent vomiting.

Respiratory

Nasal congestion, sneezing, and difficulty breathing.

B Hidden Risks & Cross-Contamination

Seafood Restaurants & Buffets

The risk of cross-contamination is very high in restaurants that serve seafood openly. Even steam from cooking fish or shellfish can trigger a reaction.

Local Flavorings

In Malaysia, be cautious of Belacan (Shrimp Paste), Cencaluk, Budu, and Oyster Sauce.

Dining Out Safety Tips:

  • Ask about the frying oil. Some restaurants use the same oil for shrimp and french fries.

  • Be careful with "Imitation" foods. Imitation crab (surimi) is usually made from white fish.
