Soy Allergy

Soy allergy often begins in infancy with reactions to soy-based formulas. While many children will outgrow it, for some individuals, it can persist into adulthood.

1 Popular Soy Products

Tofu & Tempeh

Soy Sauce

Soy Milk

Miso / Edamame

Identifying Soy on Labels

Soy is often used as an emulsifier or stabilizer. Look for these words in the ingredients:

  • ✔ Lecithin (Soy): Used in chocolates and breads.
  • ✔ Hydrolyzed Vegetable Protein (HVP): A common flavor enhancer.
  • ✔ Glycine max: The scientific name for soy.
  • ✔ Textured Vegetable Protein (TVP): A meat substitute.

Risks in Restaurants

Most cooking oils used in restaurants are Vegetable Oil, which is often a blend containing soy oil. While highly refined soy oil is considered safe for some, it still poses a risk for highly sensitive individuals.

Note: Always ask if the restaurant uses soy oil or peanut oil for frying.

Soy-Free Alternatives

Coconut Aminos

A soy sauce substitute made from coconut sap. It offers a similar salty-sweet flavor.

Almond Milk / Coconut Milk

Use as a replacement for soy milk in drinks or pastry cooking.

Chickpeas

Can be turned into 'Tofu' (Shan Tofu) without using any soy.
